## DjangoCon Europe

Took the train from Bristol to Holyhead, and the Stena Line from Holyhead to
Dublin to attend [DjangoCon Europe][djceu25]. Never got on a big ferry like that
before, so that was a cool experience. More sustainable, too!

I didn't give a full talk this year, but I did a lightning talk on Wagtail.

It's probably the most enjoyable DjangoCon Europe I've been. Maybe because
there's less pressure, but I also had a lot of fun with old friends and new.

## Global Talent visa üá¨üáß

My Skilled Worker visa (SWV) was expiring in September. So, for the first half of the year, I was busy preparing some documents to switch to the [UK Global Talent visa][uk-gtv] (GTV).

I didn't have to do this. Could've just extended my SWV. But by switching to GTV, I would be eligible for indefinite leave to remain (ILR) after living in the UK for three years.

Chuffed to say that I got it approved! Now I could apply for ILR, which I should do soon, since I only asked for 1 year of GTV.

## Personal stuff

This year has been quite a ride for me, in a few aspects. One in particular felt more like a series of rollercoaster rides.

For more than eight years, I had been holding on to a dream. I worked so hard to reach it, and there were times when it felt so close I could almost hold it. A few instances of self-sabotage and it all crumbled down. Been picking up the pieces over the past few years. Thought that was it.

A few months into the year, thought I saw signs of changes that would've opened up another chance. Leaned into it. Gave it the best shot I could.

And for a little while, there we were. Smiling at old times, thinking of new. Or so I thought.

But when each and every way either led to nowhere, a wall, or worse: a sign to turn around and not come back‚Ä¶ Much as I hated to go back on my own words, perhaps it was time to let go.

To think that it was a dream of two. When it was a dream of one.

Losing a history of my thoughts in the past five years or so was a bit unfortunate.

## What's ahead

Since last year's went pretty well, let's think about what I'd like to do in in 2026:

- **Read more books.** It's a leftover from last year's, but this time I think I'll have an easier time to get it done.
- **Write and publish more.** Still another item from last year's, but I'd like to make a bigger dent here. Maybe some personal thoughts as well. Though I'd like to see if I can keep it to a small audience. Or at least less likely to be consumed by AI. Or worse, gone to oblivion.
- **Learn something new.** Doesn't have to be tech. Doesn't have to be good at it by the end of the year. But maybe good enough to do or make something out of it.
- **Create and release one thing.** Not quite sure what it is, but it's not writing. Probably not code either. More on the artsy stuff, likely. A painting, a handicraft, a song cover, or some other thing.
- **Make a new friend.** In real life. Outside of a professional setting. I feel like my social skills have plummeted since I moved to the UK. Not sure if it's because of the language, culture, or just me. Difficult as it may be, I'd like to see it happen.

The feeling I'm getting for this year is to do things that have an impact to me personally.

Less so on the professional side. I've gone to more conferences than I ever thought I would've, and spoken at quite a few. Much as I love seeing friends at conferences, the energy (and money) it takes to attend one is not insignificant. I wouldn't mind missing a few this year. I also still have to sort out my ILR, so I have lots of reading to do.

Alright, this post has been long overdue, so I don't want to drag this out much longer. Thank you for reading this far. I hope your 2025 was delightful. Whether it was, or it wasn't, let's hope for a better year. Ta!
